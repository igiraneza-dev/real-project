student support services








Academic & Career Support
Labor Market Preparation: The center's primary mission is to empower students with hands-on skills for immediate employment in trades like Automobile Technology, Computer Systems, and Culinary Arts
Career Mentorship: As part of the Rwanda Polytechnic (RP) ecosystem, students can access career guidance, industrial mentorship, and internship programs to bridge the gap between training and the workforce
Entrepreneurship Initiatives: Students are encouraged to join Savings Clubs to develop financial management skills and support future business ownership.
Financial Assistance
While MTC is a private institution, students may be eligible for various government and partner-led financial support programs:
TVET Scholarships: The Rwanda TVET Board (RTB), in partnership with the German Government (KfW), offers full scholarship packages to high-performing students from low-income households enrolled in Technical Secondary Schools.
Education Funds: Local initiatives like the Umuhanga Education Fund provide targeted sponsorship to help students overcome financial barriers to education.
Welfare & Social Services
Youth Center Access: Students frequently use the nearby Muhanga Yego Centre (Muhanga Youth Center), which provides safe spaces for basketball, sports talent detection, and entertainment
Student Associations: Students can join professional trade associations to network socially and professionally with peers across the Rwanda Polytechnic network.

libraries
Technical Collections: The library primarily stocks materials related to the school's specialized trades, such as Automobile Technology, Culinary Arts, and Computer Systems.
Study Space: As a TVET Center, MTC provides a physical environment for technical research and study, though students often use classroom labs for hands-on learning
